🛡 Best Practices to Avoid Phishing

1. Verify the Sender

Check the email address carefully. Attackers often use domains that look similar (e.g., support@amaz0n.com instead of amazon.com).

2. Don't Click Unknown Links

Hover over links before clicking to see the actual URL. If it looks suspicious or different from what's displayed, don't click it.

3. Look for HTTPS

Legitimate sites use HTTPS. However, phishing sites can also use HTTPS, so this alone isn't enough to verify legitimacy.

4. Be Wary of Urgency

Phishing emails often create urgency ("Your account will be closed!"). Take time to verify before acting.

5. Don't Download Attachments

Unexpected attachments, especially .exe, .zip, or Office documents, can contain malware.

6. Use 2-Factor Authentication

Enable 2FA on all important accounts. Even if your credentials are compromised, attackers won't be able to access your account.

7. Report Suspicious Emails

Report phishing attempts to your email provider and the organization being impersonated.
